About Us

Band Origins

The origins of the band can be traced back to Tom and Elliot’s school band in the small ex-mining town of Ystradgynlais in the South Wales Valleys in 2010. A band covering their favourite songs from Nirvana to Chuck Berry!

Following a brief pause, the band reformed in 2015 under the name of Serene Concept. The band started playing small venues, pubs and clubs as a cover band in and around the Swansea Valley and started writing their own songs.

A number of line-up changes have taken place over the years, with Tom and Elliott ever present. The current members have clicked in a way that has sparked the success – a name change to Rapid Unscheduled Disassembly seemed right.

Having been the supporting act to a number of big names such as Stone Sour and Billy Talent in 2019, the band signed their first record deal in January 2020 and are looking forward to touring their album when restrictions on live shows are lifted.

The Band

Tom Stephens

Vocals & Rhythm Guitar

Tom first picked up a guitar aged 14 to help his friend and future bandmate, Elliott, complete a song recording for Elliot’s sisters GCSE music course. They went on to form a school band, called Serenity performing covers of their formative musical influences such as Metallica, Nirvana, Green Day, The Offspring and the Red Hot Chili Peppers. Following school, the band went their separate ways for their University Studies before recombining in 2015.

Elliott Williams

Lead Guitar & Backing Vocals

Elliott is the driving force of RUD. He is responsible for the inception and continued progress throughout, with an unrelenting drive and thirst for perfection. Elliot was in one other band during Tom and Elliot’s early hiatus, but there was no question to resume what had been started many years before. Elliot’s musical influences are vast, but he has decided on Slipknot, Bullet For My Valentine, Funeral For A Friend, The Misfits American Head Charge and Kings of Leon.

Matt Thomas

Bass Guitar

Matt joined the band in 2018 having been in a number of other local bands. After a conversation with Tom and Elliott at a gig, Matt was persuaded to jam at a practice session and the rest is history as they say! Matt brings creative but consistent bass licks and is often credited as the originator of new song ideas. Matt’s biggest musical influences are Skindred, Rise Against, System of a Down and Pink! I know, we are as perplexed as you are, but he says he stands by his claim.

Jamie Lawrence

Drums

Rapid Unscheduled Disassembly had a number of drummers before the addition of Jamie in 2017 (the last count was 10 but may be considerably higher than this). Jamie has brought a structure to the band, a steady beat some may say, but the drumming style he brings is second to none and the band have developed into a professional outfit due in no small part to Jamie’s solid beat and big drum solos. Musical influences include Foo Fighters, Queens of the Stone Age, Muse and AFI.
